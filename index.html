About F₄

What we value
Films with strong moral character, meaningful storytelling, & clear creative intent.
How it works
Awards recognize films, not individual people.
Anyone may help with any film, and contributors may participate in multiple projects or roles.

Film Categories

Feature Film
Films with a runtime of 30 minutes or more.
Narrative, documentary, or other long-form film projects.
Short Film
Films under 30 minutes.
Narrative, documentary, or experimental short-form work.
Documentary
Non-fiction films that inform, explore, investigate, or preserve meaningful real-world stories.

Awards & Judging

Judged awards are based on film craft and story impact. Audience Choice is based on viewer ratings.

Awards
Best Feature Film, Best Short Film, Best Documentary, and Audience Choice.
Story & Writing
Narrative strength, clarity, and emotional impact.
  • Story arc (setup, conflict, resolution)
  • Character development
  • Dialogue quality
  • Thematic depth
Cinematography
Visual composition and camera execution.
  • Framing and composition
  • Lighting quality
  • Camera movement
  • Visual consistency
Editing
Pacing, structure, and continuity.
  • Pacing and rhythm
  • Continuity
  • Transitions
  • Clarity of sequence
Sound & Music
Audio clarity and enhancement of story.
  • Dialogue clarity
  • Sound design
  • Music integration
  • Mixing balance
Audience Choice
Recognizes the film that most connects with the room.
  • Attendees rate films after screening
  • Ratings use a simple 1–5 scale
  • The highest qualified audience score receives the Audience Choice Award

Important Dates

Registration Closes / Submissions Open
Standard registration window closes. This gives us time to prepare, & creates a window by which we ensure that all submissions have been worked on for a meaningful period of time. At this point, submissions will be accepted.

Submission Deadline
Final day to submit eligible projects.
