Screenplay Vocabulary Guide

Use this before reading professional scripts or writing your first draft. Screenplays use special formatting so a film crew can quickly understand what appears on screen.

FADE IN:

The film begins. Often used at the start of a script.

FADE OUT:

The image fades away. Often used at the end.

INT.

Interior. The scene is inside.

EXT.

Exterior. The scene is outside.

Slugline / Scene Heading

The line that tells where and when the scene happens.

Action

What the audience can see or hear. Usually written in present tense.

Character Cue

The name of the character speaking, usually centered and capitalized.

Dialogue

The words the character says.

Parenthetical

A short note about how a line is said or what the character is doing.

V.O.

Voice-over. A voice the audience hears that is not spoken visibly in the scene.

O.S.

Off-screen. The character is in the scene but not visible.

SUPER

Text appears over the image, like a date, place, or title card.

Simple Example

FADE IN: EXT. FOREST PATH - DAY A young boy runs through the trees, clutching a torn map. JONAH Wait for me! He trips, falls, and looks back toward the sound of footsteps. SUPER: THREE HOURS EARLIER
Screenplay rule of thumb: write what the audience can see and hear. Save long explanations for your outline notes, not the screenplay page.
